The Savannah

Savannahs are flat grasslands with sparse shrubs and trees. The temperature is warm year round due to receiving more sunlight than any other environment. Savannahs only have 2 seasons, wet and dry. The wet season, or monsoon season, has 50-120cm of rainfall. There are several efficient predators in the savannah. Two of the best are the Cheetahs and African Wild Dogs.

An excellent source for more information is National Geographic


Cheetahs

Cheetahs are the fastest animal in the world able to reach speeds of up to 112 kph! Nearly all cheetahs can be found in sub-Saharan Africa, however they can also be found in northeastern Iran.
There are only a few dozen that reside there. These beautiful feline predators have a pale yellow coat with black dots on the upper parts, and are white on the underbelly.
Their faces can be distinguished by distinctive black lines from the inner corner of each eye to the outer corners of the mouth.

For more information on cheetahs, visit National Geographic

African Wild Dogs


An African Wild Dog is one of the most efficient predators on Earth. In a pack, they are even more dangerous. These animals travel in packs as small as 10, to as big as 40.
These animals are pack predators hunting antelope, gazelle, and even wildebeasts. These dogs can sprint at speeds of 70 mph. These efficient predators are endangered.

For more information on these pack predators, visit WWF
